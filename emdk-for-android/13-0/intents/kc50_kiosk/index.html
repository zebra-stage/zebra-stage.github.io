Zebra KC50 Kiosk

EMDK For Android 13.0

Overview

Zebra's KC50 is a rugged Android-based fixed-mount computer designed for kiosk applications including point-of-sale, information display and/or look-up, and various payment and conversational self-service scenarios. Available in 22-inch non-touch, and 15-inch touch and non-touch models, the KC-50 can be wall-mounted or stand alone using floor- or counter-mounted accessories.

The 15-inch KC50 with optional back-to-back counter stand taking NFC payment at point of sale. Click image to enlarge; ESC to exit.

The KC50 supports Power over Ethernet and can be expanded to include scanners, LED indicators, payment terminals and other accessories to suit interactive use cases in airports, hospitality, retail, healthcare and many others. The unit is IP65-rated to protect it from ingress of dust, debris and liquids in kitchens and other hostile environments.

KC50 Series Notes

  • A list of compatible Power Sourcing Equipment (PSE) can be found in the online Product Reference Guide.
  • Zebra recommends testing PoE in a customer “lab setting” with minimum Cat5e RJ45 cabling before full deployment to ensure that PoE equipment is compatible.
  • Zebra recommends a qualified cable installer test the RJ45 cabling at each site to ensure they are wired properly per TIA 586.2-D standards.
  • The detection and discovery of the PoE power class (4,6,8) occurs early during boot-up of the KC50 device.
  • A flashing red LED indicates it has not detected a suitable power class for this product (Minimum Class 4).
  • LG release in November will allow customers to download json files (customized to their use case). These fixed tables do not allow mix-and-match of USB port enable/disabling.
